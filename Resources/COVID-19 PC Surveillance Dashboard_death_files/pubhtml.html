Epidemic Evaluation Index (EEI)The EEI indicates the epidemic situation, it can be used to monitor and evaluate the evolution of an epidemic over time.
If the EEI is higher than 1.0, it suggests that the epidemic is still developing.
If the EEI drops below 1.0, then the epidemic may have entered the period of decline.
If the EEI remains below 1.0 for more than 7 days, it indicates stable remission period.
Source: https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.03.24.20027730v2

Reproduction Rate (Rt)Reproduction Rate (Rt)Reproduction Rate (Rt) is defined as the average number of people who become infected by an infectious person. It is an indicator of how fast the virus is spreading. Statistical model shows the R0 estimate with upper and lower confidence limits. If Rt is above 1.0, the virus will spread quickly. When Rt is below 1.0, the virus will stop spreading. Rt projects were calculated for the United States, California, San Bernardino County, Riverside, Los Angeles, Orange County, and San Diego County.
